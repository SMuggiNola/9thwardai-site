ACT Prep Pathway

Tier 1: Essentials Track (Approx. Goal: 23+)

  • Focus: Work on Integrating Essential Skills — fractions, percentages, ratios, slope, linear equations, coordinate plane basics, and interpreting graphs/tables.
  • Rationale: These questions account for the largest share of ACT Math — roughly 25 of the 60 problems (about 40% of the test). Building accuracy here creates the strongest foundation for overall growth.
  • Approach: Short practice sets with repeated review, emphasizing accuracy and consistency in the most frequently tested skills.
  • Example: A store marks up the price of a $40 jacket by 25%. During a sale, the store takes 20% off the new price. What is the final price of the jacket?

Tier 2: Challenge Track (Approx. Goal: 27+)

  • Entry: For students who have shown consistent progress on Essentials.
  • Focus: Higher-level Challenge Standards — quadratics, systems of equations, polynomial/rational functions, circle geometry, and introductory trigonometry.
  • Approach: Extended practice with multi-step problems, targeted feedback, and enrichment aimed at advanced algebra and precalculus content.

Overall Strategy

Students begin with the Essentials Track to strengthen core skills. Those who demonstrate readiness can take on the Challenge Track, which adds higher-level content to support further growth. This two-tier model keeps practice focused and gives students clear next steps as they improve.
